Career as a web developer


A web developers design and create websites. They develop client services that is what a user interacts with, the backend that holds user information such as login information, and the website logic that connects the user and the backend.

Job Description


A web developers design and create websites. They develop client services that is what a user interacts with, the backend that holds user information such as login information, and the website logic that connects the user and the backend.

Salary


According to the BLS the average pay of a web developer is around $67,000

Working Conditions

Resource: Working conditions cover a broad range of topics and issues, from working time (hours of work, rest periods, and work schedules) to remuneration, as well as the physical conditions and mental demands that exist in the workplace. i

Developers work schedule is usually 9am to 5pm, most jobs are salaried, or contract paid, that eliminates overtime and work hour restrictions, so developers can work late hours or get stared early in the mornings. Some developers are on a 24hr call to respond to any emergencies. The job is not physically demanding, however working on the computer all day could cause backache, repetitive-strain injury, or visual problems. Developers usually work in casual environments, they dress down and work in groups. (Collegegrad.com, 2019)


Education Requirements

Most employers require a Bachelor’s degree or equivalent work experience, however alternative training such as online courses and web design boot camps that offer hands on training are preparing students for entry level positions

Required knowledge and skills include; HTML, CSS, JavaScript and any Object Oriented prohraming language with skills in Database Management Systems, jQuery and Mobile Web Development


Job Outlook

According to the BLS, jobs are projected to grow 15 percent from 2016 to 2026, a much faster rate than the average for all occupations due to demand in technology. (Bls.gov, 2018)


Overall Pros and Cons

Some pros include causal work environment that includes dress code, office environment, some jobs offer perks such as game boards, and a full-service cafes and bars. The job is high paying, and great for team building. Has fewer traveling opportunities, may cause visual problems and back problems. Has less growth opportunities


Advancement Opportunities

Web developers have limited growth opportunities, they can advance to manager junior developers


Who can become a web developer

Anyone can become a web developer. You don’t need to be a tech wizard or possess a never-ending list of formal qualifications; as long as you’re passionate about the field and willing to learn, a career in web development is well within your reach.

A web developer job requires a creativity, detailed oriented person. You also need high concentration skills and customer service skills. If you can do that, this job is for you.
